User Study

Here you can explore key findings from a University of Glasgow MSc Project Study on public perceptions of AI-generated music, with a focus on how younger generations view its ethical and legal aspects. These insights provide a fresh perspective, complementing existing research.

General Knowledge & Awareness

A significant portion of respondents (55%) are at least somewhat familiar with the application of AI in music creation, but 25% are not familiar at all. This indicates a moderate level of awareness, with potential for further education on AI's role in the music industry.

Rare Use of AI in Music Creation Processes?

Many respondents underestimate the role of AI in music creation processes, with many believing AI is rarely used in areas like composing new music, mastering, production, and lyric writing, despite its prevalence. This highlights a gap between the actual use of AI in music creation and public perception.

Future & Responsible Practices

An overwhelming 90% of respondents believe AI will play a significant role in music creation over the next decade, either as a co-creator or an autonomous creator.

A strong majority (85%) believe that specific regulations are necessary to govern AI in music creation, indicating a widespread call for structured oversight.

Key Legal & Ethical Priorities

according to respondents

Based on the survey results, respondents consistently rated these few practices as important or very important for addressing legal and ethical issues in AI-generated music: clear labeling of AI-generated content, proper licensing for copyrighted music in AI training, appropriate credit for original artists, strong ethical guidelines, fair compensation for artists whose work is used in AI, and public education to raise awareness about AI's role in music.
